Privacy notice

This page provides details of what personal data the Department for Education (DfE) processes during the use of Ed chat about our workforce including:

  • current and former DfE and agency employees and workers
  • public appointments
  • board members
  • non-executive directors
  • ministers and contractors

This notice:

  • can be updated at any time, please check back for updates

When we collect and use your personal information, we follow the relevant laws including:

  • Data Protection Act (DPA)
  • UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR)

We must have a valid reason to collect your personal data. These reasons are called lawful basis in UK GDPR. Your rights under UK GDPR about how your personal data is collected and used may change depending on the lawful basis for collecting the data.

Further information on the standards you can expect when we collect, hold or use your personal information is available on the personal information charter.

1. Using your data when you use Ed chat

1.1 Purpose and lawful basis for processing

For the purposes of relevant data protection legislation, the DfE is the data controller for personal data processed by DfE.

Our purpose for processing this information is to enable you to log in and use Ed Chat. Storing your name enables you to access your previous chats up to 30 working days after they were created.

The lawful basis we rely on for this processing of your personal data is legitimate interest, under article 6(1)(f) of the UK GDPR.

1.2 Data collected

DfE collects and uses the following personal information including your:

  • name

1.3 What we do with your data

We need your personal data to:

  • ensure continued access for users to the service
  • keep a dashboard to monitor the performance and usage of the service

1.4 How long we keep your data

We keep your personal information for 30 working days in the service. We do not keep any personal data on the dashboard.

1.5 Your information rights under UK GDPR

Find out about your rights when we process your personal data under the lawful basis of legitimate interest.

1.6 Data processors

A data processor is an organisation that processes your information on DfE’s behalf. We do not use any data processors for this processing activity.

1.7 How to contact us and make a complaint

Requesting your personal information from DfE has information on how you can:

  • ask questions about how we use your information
  • make a complaint to the Data Protection Officer and the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)

1.8 How to access your personal information

You can make a request to access your personal information verbally or in writing.

Contact us

If you have questions about this notice or how we use your data, contact the DfE Data Protection Officer:

Email: dpo@education.gov.uk
